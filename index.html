G5 · Present Participle verb + -ing
📌 Formel: Verb der Wahrnehmung + Person/Sache + verb-ing

Benutze das Present Participle nach diesen Verben:

VerbBedeutungBeispiel
see / watch / noticesehen / beobachtenI saw the car driving away.
hear / listen tohörenShe heard him singing.
feelfühlenHe felt the rain getting heavier.
smellriechenI smelled something burning.
💡 Trick: Zwei Sätze → Ein Satz!
"A boy was shouting. I heard him." → I heard a boy shouting.
⚠️ Sonderregeln beim -ing:
• Verb endet auf -e → e wegfallen lassen: come → coming
• Kurzer Vokal + Konsonant → Konsonant verdoppeln: run → running

Mehr Beispiele:

We watched the children playing in the park.
Did you hear Ryan singing in the shower?
Kate didn't notice the people leaving the building.

Signalwörter:

SignalwortDeutschBeispiel

G7 · Passive Voice Simple Past was/were + 3. Form
📌 Formel: was/were + past participle (3. Form)
💡 Wann? Wenn die Handlung wichtiger ist als die Person, die sie tut.
SingularPlural
The diamond was found.The diamonds were found.
Apartheid was abolished.Many buildings were built.

Aktiv → Passiv:

Aktiv: The British invaded South Africa.
Passiv: South Africa was invaded by the British.
Aktiv: They abolished apartheid in 1991.
Passiv: Apartheid was abolished in 1991.
💡 Mit "by" kannst du sagen, wer die Handlung ausgeführt hat – aber nur wenn es wichtig ist!

Fragen im Passive:

Was the park opened in 1927? → Yes, it was. / No, it wasn't.
Were the diamonds found in the 19th century? → Yes, they were.
